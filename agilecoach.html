Agile should not be complicated but it often seems that way...

I am an advocate of Agnostic Agile, encouraging clients to adopt Essence as a way of working, taking what works for them from existing processes and the various frameworks.
My tips for a successful Agile project are:
  • Communication is key
    • Don't rely on email, get face to face where possible, use video or instant messaging if necessary
    • Keep documentation to a barely sufficient minimum
    • Where documention is required, use collaborative tools to maintain a single source of truth
  • Be inclusive, include a representative of all stakeholder groups in your team
    • Product Owner
    • Business Analyst
    • Customer/End User
    • Development
    • QA
    • Operations (if you embrace DevOps too - which you should!)
  • Set sustainable goals
    • Know your team's velocity
    • Set a WIP limit
    • Embrace change
  • Deliver frequently
    • Define a release frequency or cadence that you can maintain
    • Demonstrate value with each release

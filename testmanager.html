Quality Assurance

Modern software delivery is more dependent on testing than ever before, to ensure that increasingly frequent releases deliver quality to ever more demanding customers.

Quality should not only be also be considered at the end of the development cycle. Instead all stages from design through to deployment. The industry buzzwords for this is Shift Left, referring to introducing quality earlier or further left in the cycle.

With experience of QA in diverse sectors, we can help you understand how to introduce quality assurance techniques in your organisation. Some examples of Mike's previous projects are:

  • Data Migration - reconciliation of customer billing data at the Extract phase of a large ETL project maximised the quantity and quality of data migrated from a propietary billing system to Oracle CC&B for a large public utility.
  • Mobile phone network - introducing quality from the point a change is identified (live bug fix, new offering or service enhancement), dramatically reduced the failure rate for a major UK mobile phone network.
  • Logistics & Distribution - working with the PMO to consider quality from requirements gathering onwards helped a major UK retailer improve project delivery.
  • Ecommerce - Embedding test engineers into Agile teams has helped a number of major UK Ecommerce retailers to maintain quality with a high release velocity.
